# Paper 2 claim map (working, foundation-aware)

## Scope guardrail
Paper 2 does **not** attempt to prove from scratch that robust ordering exists in the Helmholtz–Robin model. That foundational claim belongs to Paper 1. Paper 2 begins from that established basis.

## Main claims
1. **Inherited basis from Paper 1**: the 3D Helmholtz–Robin field supports artifact-hardened ordering on cube-like and lattice-linked geometries.
2. **Field-core organization sharpens under Robin variation**: the strongest early Paper-2 claims concern family-level organization under beta-variation rather than brittle fine labels.
3. **Phase-family organization is robust enough to survive multiple hardening tests**: null models, readout variation, and family-restricted diagnostics support the claim that the ordering is not just an artifact of a single readout convention.
4. **Opposite-locking, carrier-shell, and CEF diagnostics form a geometric bridge from field-core ordering toward later detector modeling.**
5. **A reduced field-core description can be extracted** through minimal-kernel, carrier-graph, bridge-geometry, and transition-zone diagnostics.
6. **The double-slit continuation is stepwise rather than ad hoc**: one-slit baseline, coherent double slit, measurement surrogates, composite detector model, detector matrix, then minimal detector law.
7. **The effective detector law is supported by preregistered matrix outputs, external validation, and holdout-style outputs.**

## Repo / paper separation
- `repo only`: full artifact inventory, working reports, duplicates, exploratory notes
- `appendix / supplement`: extended bridge diagnostics and reduction details
- `main text`: inherited Paper-1 basis, field-core hardening, bridge logic, detector matrix, minimal detector law
