# Forschungshistorie

This note gives a compact reader-facing history of how the analyses in this repository were assembled.

## Starting point
The repository does not begin from a blank field model. It starts from the validated basis established in the earlier companion manuscript:

**Reproducible Low-Mode Ordering in a 3D Helmholtz–Robin Eigenproblem on Cube-Like and Lattice-Linked Geometries**

That earlier work established robust low-mode ordering and the basic readout/null-model discipline. The present repository begins where that foundation ends.

## Phase A — from validated ordering to spectral organization
The first task was not yet to simulate the double slit. It was to determine how the already validated field ordering reorganizes under Robin variation. Dense beta scans, branch tracking, readout robustness, and subspace / axis tests showed that the stable structure lives at family level rather than in fragile axis labels.

## Phase B — null models and phase-sensitive sharpening
Once family-level organization became visible, the next step was to attack it with null models and phase-sensitive diagnostics. These analyses tested whether the apparent organization survived against deliberately weakened or phase-scrambled alternatives.

## Phase C — opposite, carrier, and corner-edge-face structure
The next block translated earlier geometric intuitions into explicit tests. Opposite-pair locking, carrier shells, and corner-edge-face incidence were treated as measurable field-organization hypotheses rather than as informal visualization impressions.

## Phase D — reduction to a minimal field-core description
After many diagnostics were accumulated, the repository moved toward reduction rather than expansion. The goal became to extract a smaller field-core description that still preserved the robust organizational backbone needed for the later measurement-geometry analyses.

## Phase E — stepwise measurement geometry
Only after the field core had been organized and reduced did the repository move into slit geometries. The build-up was gradual: one-slit baselines, coherent double-slit baselines, surrogate disturbance models, explicit Robin-variation tests, and then more realistic detector bundles.

## Phase F — effective detector law and microscopic support
The final stage reduced the detector response to a compact effective law and then tested whether that law admitted a plausible microscopic Robin-noise continuation. This is the point where the repository transitions from field-core organization into measurement geometry and model-based experimental predictions.

## Function of this note
This history is included only to orient readers. The repository itself is organized as a companion reproducibility pipeline for the manuscript:

**Spectral Organization of a 3D Helmholtz–Robin Field Core and Its Transition into Measurement Geometry**
